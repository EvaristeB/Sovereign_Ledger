# Sovereign Ledger - Gameplay Guide

## First Steps: Getting Started

### 1. Create Your Empire
When you connect to the server, you create an empire with:
- **Name**: Your empire's name (e.g., "Solar Dynasty")
- **Starting Credit**: 100,000 credits (configurable)
- **Home Planet**: A starting terrestrial planet with basic resources

### 2. Control Your Resources
Your treasury is everything. Every action costs credits:
- **Mining**: Deploy survey probes, then extraction fleet
- **Refining**: Build refinery on colony, allocate workers
- **Manufacturing**: Combine resources into higher-value goods
- **Trade**: Buy/sell on market or negotiate direct deals

### 3. Build Your Trade Network
The economy IS the game. Your goal is to:
1. Identify profitable resources in your sector
2. Extract them efficiently
3. Transport to high-demand markets
4. Sell for maximum profit
5. Repeat with reinvested profits

## Winning Conditions

### Path 1: **Monopoly Master**
Control 90%+ of a vital resource across all known systems.

**Strategy**:
- Identify one high-demand resource (e.g., antimateria)
- Mine all deposits aggressively
- Buy out competitors with treasury advantage
- Control supply → control prices

**Counter**: Enemies embargo your resource, find substitutes

---

### Path 2: **Economic Supremacy**
Literally buy your enemies' surrender through reparations.

**Strategy**:
- Run most profitable trade routes
- Accumulate massive treasury (billions of credits)
- Use wealth to:
  - Outbid competitors for resources
  - Sponsor conflicts between rivals
  - Force peace treaties with reparations

**Counter**: Players form alliances to stop you

---

### Path 3: **Technological Dominance**
Make competitors' manufacturing impossibly expensive through monopolies.

**Strategy**:
- Control all rare components needed for production
- Force everyone to buy from your refineries
- Technology advancement becomes their bottleneck
- They can't build ships/factories because you control inputs

**Counter**: Others find alternative production chains

---

### Path 4: **Cultural/Market Dominance**
Make your products indispensable.

**Strategy**:
- Produce the best food, fuel, components
- Every empire depends on your goods
- Price near profitability ceiling
- Your profit margin becomes the economy's profit margin

**Counter**: Embargo, blockade, or military conquest

---

## Economic Warfare Tactics

### 1. Market Flooding
Dump massive quantities of a resource to crash its price.
- **Goal**: Bankrupt competitors who depend on that resource
- **Cost**: Lose money short-term to profit long-term
- **Counter**: Competitors diversify, find substitutes

### 2. Supply Chain Disruption
Control a critical middle component.
- **Example**: Control all Circuits production
- **Result**: No one can build factories or ships
- **Duration**: Until someone breaks your monopoly

### 3. Blockade Starvation
Position fleet on enemy trade routes.
- **Effect**: 50% cargo loss if they try to pass
- **Cost**: Fleet maintenance while blockading
- **Counter**: Military navies destroy blockade fleet

### 4. Embargo
Refuse to sell vital resources to target empire.
- **Effect**: They can't produce without finding alternatives
- **Duration**: Sustained pressure over 20+ cycles
- **Counter**: Enemies embargo you back

### 5. Proxy Warfare
Hire pirates/rebels with payment to attack specific targets.
- **Cost**: 1,000-5,000 credits per raid
- **Effect**: 30% cargo loss or ship destruction
- **Plausible Deniability**: No official war

### 6. Hostile Takeover
Buy majority stake in competing empire's assets.
- **Targets**: His richest trade route or colony
- **Method**: Offer reparations/tributes in peace treaties
- **Result**: Control their production or wealth

## Strategic Phases

### Early Game (Cycles 1-100)
**Focus**: Establish basic production and trade

- Survey 3-5 nearby systems
- Build 2-3 mines and 1 refinery
- Establish first profitable trade route
- Accumulate 50,000 credit surplus

**Danger**: Bankruptcy if inefficient

---

### Mid Game (Cycles 100-500)
**Focus**: Economic dominance and competition

- Control 3-5 planets/colonies
- Run 5-10 trade routes simultaneously
- Achieve 500,000+ credit treasury
- Begin targeting specific resources

**Danger**: Rivals consolidate power

---

### Late Game (Cycles 500+)
**Focus**: Execute end-game strategy

- Pick one victory condition
- Position for monopoly/supremacy/war
- Eliminate competition through economic means
- Defend against rival coalitions

**Danger**: Multiple enemies coordinating

---

## Scenario Examples

### Scenario 1: "The Iron Crisis"
**Cycle 1**: Iron prices are stable at 100 credits
**Cycle 50**: A new player floods the market with 1,000,000 tonnes of iron
**Cycle 51**: Iron crashes to 30 credits
**Effect**: All steel refineries become unprofitable, factories halt
**Winner**: The player who bought 500,000 iron at high prices now sells at monopoly prices

### Scenario 2: "The Antimateria War"
**Cycle 200**: Two rivals both need antimateria for late-game fleets
**Cycle 210**: Antimateria price: 20,000 credits (supply exhausted)
**Cycle 220**: War declared (economic forcing creates military conflict)
**Cycle 230**: Fleet battle; loser loses 10,000 credits per destroyed ship
**Winner**: Whoever controls antimateria supply surrenders for reparations

### Scenario 3: "The Trade Network Collapse"
**Cycle 300**: Empire A controls 80% of Fuel supply
**Cycle 310**: Empire A blockades trade routes
**Cycle 320**: Empire B's fleets become immobile (no fuel)
**Cycle 330**: Empire A forces surrender terms
**Outcome**: B pays 100,000 credits/cycle tribute to A

## Tips by Playstyle

### The **Tycoon** (Profit-Maximizing)
- Identify price arbitrage opportunities
- Buy low in one sector, sell high in another
- Scale with reinvested profits
- Avoid military conflict

### The **Logistician** (Efficiency-Focused)
- Optimize routes for maximum throughput
- Minimize fuel costs through tech research
- Establish redundant supply chains
- Profit through superior execution

### The **Speculator** (Risk-Taking)
- Make big bets on market crashes/booms
- Use leverage (buy futures)
- Horizontal monopolies of single resources
- Boom→Bust→Rebound cycles

### The **Militarist** (Conquest-Focused)
- Build powerful fleets early
- Use force to demand tribute
- Control strategic chokepoints
- War as economic policy

### The **Saboteur** (Disruption-Focused)
- Identify key competitors' bottlenecks
- Target their supply chains
- Use market warfare and piracy
- Win through others' bankruptcy

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-expansion**: Too many colonies before establishing income
2. **Single dependency**: Relying on one trade route
3. **Ignoring tech**: Outdated ships/refineries become unprofitable
4. **War without treasury**: Can't sustain military costs
5. **Ignoring market trends**: Missing crashes and boom cycles
6. **Trusting enemies**: Allies abandon you in hard times

## Economics Cheat Sheet

```
Profit = Revenue - Costs
Revenue = Sell Price × Quantity
Costs = (Buy Price × Qty) + Shipping + Maintenance

Shipping = distance × weight × 0.001 × (1 - techBonus)
Fuel = distance/100 × fleet size multiplier × cargo modifier

Market Price = Base Price × Demand/Supply elasticity
Tech Effect = 1 + (tech_level × 0.1) = 10% per level advantage

Break-even = costs_per_cycle / (profit_per_unit × units_per_cycle)
```

---

**Remember**: In Sovereign Ledger, the player with the best understanding of supply chains wins. Not the one with the biggest fleet.
